Prepare workspace

This entails thoroughly cleaning your desk to ensure it is clear of dust and clutter. Particles in the environment can cause mechanical components to fail. Moving any liquids or clutter is generally self-evident, but a dust-free atmosphere is ideal.

Tools

  • Screwdriver (Philips head)
  • Zip ties

Yep, these tools are enough to build your PC!

Safety

  1. Before working on your computer, always disconnect and unplug any power supplies.
  2. Even after the power has been turned off, some parts can store charge. Allow some time for these to spontaneously release.
  3. Individual elements, such as your power supply or monitor, should never be disassembled.
  4. Sharp edges on your computer case should be avoided as they might cause wounds or harm.

Components required to build a PC

  1. Processor (CPU)
  2. Motherboard (MOBO)
  3. Power Supply Unit (PSU)
  4. Graphic Card (GPU)
  5. Storage (SSD or HDD)
  6. Memory (RAM)
  7. PC Case
  8. Peripherals

Not sure what these components are?

  1. Processor (CPU) - The central processing unit (CPU) is responsible for executing the instructions that make up a computer program. This is the heart of your computer.
  2. Motherboard (MOBO) - The MOBO connects the hardware components of your computer.
  3. Power Supply Unit (PSU) – The power supply (PSU) is responsible for supplying power to the system.
  4. Graphic Card (GPU) - The graphical processing unit (GPU) is the most significant component of a gaming PC because it renders visuals.
  5. Storage (SSD or HDD) - Your operating system, apps, games, and media files are all stored on your SSD/ HDD.
  6. Memory (RAM) - RAM (random access memory) is temporary data that the computer is currently using.
  7. PC Case – To cover all your system hardware in a secure cage.
  8. Peripherals - Additional features including software (OS), monitor, keyboard, mouse, etc.

Procedure to build PC:

Step 1: Open case

  1. Remove side panels
  2. Remove all packaging from case such as cables and screws.

Step 2: Install Motherboard (MOBO)

  1. Insert your I/O shield into the back of the case's cut-out.
  2. Sit the motherboard on top of the motherboard standoffs.
  3. Screw the motherboard standoffs into case’s mounting points.

Step 3: Install Processor (CPU)

  1. Find the CPU Socket on the motherboard.
  2. Raise the lever upright and open the socket cover (if you have intel) to install the CPU.
  3. Now match the arrow on CPU with the mark on CPU socket as this indicates the correct positioning.
  4. Gently sit the CPU into the socket and lower the lever to hold the CPU securely.

Step 4: Install Fan

  1. Remove the front panel from the case.
  2. Line the small arrow shown on the fan up depending on the direction of airflow you wish.
  3. Screw the fan in its place.

Step 5: Install CPU Cooler

  1. Apply thermal paste behind the CPU if not pre-applied already.
  2. Then place the cooler on top of CPU, making sure screw holes are aligned properly.
  3. Screw it in.

Step 6: Install Storage (SSD or HDD)

  1. Place the storage drivers into the case drive bays.
  2. Screw the holes in.
  3. Use the SATA cable to connect the driver to the motherboard.
  4. Connect the storage drive's power cable.

Step 7: Install Memory (RAM)

  1. Open the clips for inserting RAM on the motherboard.
  2. Push in the memory stick with notches aligned to RAM and slot.

Step 8: Install Graphics Card (GPU)

  1. Remove any plastic from the graphics card and expansion slot covers from the rear of the case where the graphics card is to be inserted.
  2. Then line it up and push it gently into the PCI expansion slot on the motherboard.
  3. Screw in the holes to keep the graphics card in place.

Step 9: Install Power Supply (PSU)

  1. Plug in the required cables according to the labels on PSU.
  2. Screw the power supply to the case mounting points and tighten them.

Step 10: Connect your Peripherals

Finally with all steps completed above, now you can connect your monitor, keyboard, mouse, speakers, network, install OS and other things you desire. Happy Gaming!
